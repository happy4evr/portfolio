SAP Field Trip

Throughout the field trips I have attended, one that stood out the most was my trip to SAP which was quite intriguing in terms other employees sharing their experience towards becomes successful in life. While visiting the various departments in SAP, I recall an employee explaining how the way to success isn’t based off of the number of A’s or B’s one gets, rather it’s the result of one’s determination and effort applied into their education. Also, I gained some helpful tips towards becoming a computer software engineer such as starting internships and opening clubs to get a better understanding of the work criteria along with building leadership skills which will help communicate with others. Likewise, I learned about the fundamentals SAP focuses upon with regard to the different areas in business such as marketing, engineering, processing, food industry, etc. With that being said, this field trip is quite important and memorable due to the welcoming hosts along with the knowledge obtained.

Guest Speaker

Accordingly, when taken into consideration of all the guest speakers, the most influential presentation was the “Professional Engineering Licensing with Emphasis on Civil Engineering” by Dan Cherner and Natalina Dernarai. The overall concepts being discussed were based on the steps to get into the civil engineering pathway along with some facts related towards the history of this particular job. In addition, I want to become a computer software engineer and what I found quite intriguing in this presentation was that the presenters took into account of all the possible jobs and spoke about engineering as a whole. In other words, Dan and Natalina didn’t only go over the civil engineering aspect, they included facts about what makes a good engineer in terms of being responsible, independent, and determined. Also, what I found quite inspiring regarding this specific career is the way buildings are made with regard to the correct proportion required because if the building isn’t equally balanced, it simply collapses which wastes time, money, and the effort spent on it. Another factor to consider is that in order for projects to function in the civil engineering field, there needs to be a civil, mechanical, along with an electrical engineer present at all times.
